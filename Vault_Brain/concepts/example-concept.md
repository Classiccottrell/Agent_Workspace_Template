An atomic note captures a single idea in its own file, written so it stands on its own without the surrounding context that produced it. Keeping notes atomic makes them easy to link from many places and easy to recombine into new arguments later. The cost is up front — you split ideas as you write — but retrieval and reuse pay it back.
