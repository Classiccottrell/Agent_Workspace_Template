## Summary
Mom-and-Pop SaaS represents a software category where domain experts with little or no technical background create specialized, niche applications using AI-driven software generation platforms. Rather than aiming for high-growth venture backing, these projects focus on productizing industry-specific workflows and knowledge to address targeted markets. Enabled by platforms like Lovable, this movement transitions the AI narrative from developer productivity to broader economic participation.

## Key Facts
- **Definition**: Mom-and-Pop SaaS refers to highly profitable, niche-focused software businesses built by domain experts (e.g., accountants, teachers, recruiters) solving specific, local problems.
- **Economic Participation**: Rather than just a developer productivity story, AI-driven software creation acts as a democratizing force, enabling non-technical professionals to build and monetize software.
- **Builder Demographics**: A platform study by Lovable revealed that 80% of software builders come from non-technical backgrounds, and 55% possess over 11 years of professional experience, indicating that domain expertise is replacing technical skills.
- **Monetization & Profitability**: In the builder economy, 80% of creators intend to monetize their applications and 35% are already generating revenue. Common software targets include business operations, consumer apps, and analytics dashboards.
- **Jevons Paradox**: As AI reduces the cost and complexity of building software to near zero, it triggers the Jevons Paradox—leading to a massive expansion of the software economy rather than a decrease in software consumption.
- **Venture Capital Shift**: Mom-and-Pop SaaS success is defined by sustainable cash flow and niche market fit, sitting in the "sweet spot" between personal hobby projects and VC-backed enterprise applications.
- **Gender Gap**: Currently, only 14% of builders on Lovable are women, highlighting a participation gap that initiatives like SheBuilds hackathons aim to close.
