## Summary
Knowledge management is the practice of capturing, organizing, and retrieving information so that what you learn stays useful over time. Personal knowledge management (PKM) applies this to one person's notes, favoring small atomic notes that link to each other rather than long isolated documents. This vault implements PKM through the [[llm-wiki-pattern]], using [[obsidian]] as the editor and [[claude-code]] as the maintainer.

## Key Facts
- Atomic notes — one idea per note — are easier to reuse and link than long documents.
- Linking notes turns a flat folder into a navigable graph, surfacing connections you would otherwise forget.
- A capture-then-process workflow (raw inbox → distilled concept → linked wiki page) prevents notes from piling up unread.
- Retrieval matters as much as capture: a note you cannot find again is a note you did not keep.
- The "evergreen" principle: revisit and refine notes so they improve rather than decay.
